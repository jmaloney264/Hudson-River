A Fresh Start For A Famously Filthy River?

An EPA Superfund sees progress and continuing challenges in cleaning up a national icon

11/24/2024

The Hudson River is arguably the most famously polluted river in the United States, where centuries of sewage, litter, and industrial waste have created a body of water that has never been swimmable of fishable within living memory. Among the potent cocktail of toxins to be found in this iconic river, PCBS, or polychlorinated biphenyls, have consistently ranked among the most toxic and prevalent, to the extent that the EPA has dedicated a 20-year Superfund whose main purpose is to remove PCBs from the Hudson River. Their main tool to achieve this has been though dredging, by simply removing contaminated sediment from the river bed and allowing the river to gradually wash itself clean. This chart shows PCP concentrations throughout the Hudson River before (2004-2008)-, during (2009-2015), and after (2016-2021) the dredging periods:

As can be clearly seen by this chart, this simplistic, and more to the point cost effective method has seen mixed results. While PCP levels mostly declined after the dredging, there was a huge spike in the toxins during the dredging process as PCB’s were released from the riverbed and directly into the river itself. Years after the dredging was completed, some regions even saw higher PCP concentrations than before the dredging due to the increased presence of polluted sediment.

According to the Riverkeeper, the EPA’s have done nearly enough to remove PCB contamination from the river, and the Hudson is still nowhere close to being used for recreational purposes, such as fishing or swimming. According to Riverkeeper’s Senior Attorney Drew Gamill, “in the cleanest parts of the river, the EPA’s stated goal is to allow for a healthy adult to be able to eat one fish per week from the river. They are nowhere close to this, and as it stands now you can barely eat 1 fish per month. The EPA is trying to conduct this operation the cheapest and quickest way possible, and it shows.”
